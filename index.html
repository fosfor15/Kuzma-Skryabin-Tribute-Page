Andriy Kuzmenko

Also known as Kuzma Skryabin, he is the legendary Ukrainian musician with strong political position and high energetic lifestyle

Here is timeline biography of Andriy Kuzmenko's life

  • 17 August 1968 – Andriy Kuzmenko was born in Sambir, Lviv region, Ukranian SSR.
  • His family moved to the city of Novoyavorivsk. As a child, he dreamed of becoming a garbage truck driver.
  • Andriy Kuzmenko graduated from music school and from the Faculty of Dentistry. He was not interested in dentistry, but finished his studies at the request of the parents.
  • 14 September 1997 – Andriy Kuzmenko and his wife Svitlana had a daughter, who was given the name Maria Barbara. That was made in honor of the Polish actress Barbara Brylska, a childhood idol of Kuzmenko.
  • 2000 – Became a presenter of his own hit parade "Hot Seven", which lasted until 2002.
  • 2001 – Starred in the very famous ukrainian New Year film "Evenings in Khutir near Dykanka", written by Nikolai Gogol in 1829–32.
  • 2003 – Worked as a presenter of the programs "Chance" and "Shikanemo" with singer Natalia Mogilevska.
  • 2006 – Published his autobiography, titled "Me, Pobeda and Berlin". The book was wildly popular, and has been reprinted several times since then.
  • 2009 – Was a voting member for the "Book of the Year B-B-C" award. Created the group "Payushchie Trusy" (Singing Pants), and helped the project "DZIDZIO" grow in popularity.
  • 2014 – Recorded a song dedicated to the events of Euromaidan, stating that he supports the people on the Maidan, but not the opposition.
  • During the 2014 war, he helped the military and was wounded in the area of ATO in eastern Ukraine.
  • Shortly before his death, Kuzmenko strongly criticized the current government in interviews, accusing them of corruption.
  • 2 February 2015 – Andriy Kuzmenko was returning from Kryvyi Rih, where he had concert, to Kyiv to get into airport and go abroad with his family for regular travelling. The road was slippery from rain and frost, at about 7 AM on a long road bend near Lozuvatka village in Kryvyi Rih Raion his car "Toyota Sequoia" collided with a GAZ-5312 milk tanker. Despite the deployed airbags, Andriy Kuzmenko died a few hours later on the spot from his injuries.

The after days

  • 20 May 2015 – A tribute concert entitled “Scriabin. Concert of memory" was performed in Kyiv. More than ten thousand people came to listen to the concert and honor the memory of the idol. The songs of the group together with the musicians of "Scriabin" were performed by famous musicians.
  • In Lutsk took place opening of the monument dedicated to Andrey Kuzmenko
  • 29 October 2015 – Awarded the Order of Merit I Class
  • Summer 2018 – Ukrposhta issued a postage stamp “Andrey Kuzmenko. 1968-2015" to the 50th anniversary of the birth with the image of the musician. The stamp was issued with a circulation of 130 thousand copies, its face value is 9 hryvnia.
  • In Kyiv and Kryvyi Rih appeared public gardens named after Kuzma Skryabin, a public garden is being built in Kramatorsk.
  • 17 August 2020 – Awarded the title of Hero of Ukraine

The author's opinion

Andriy Kuzmenko was the true patriot of his Motherland with strong political position, that unfortunately he had no time to implement. Although the history does not tolerate the subjunctive mood, if Andrey Kuzmenko were alive, Ukraine could be a completely different country.
